PP434 Project

Pathways to High Human Development in India

An empirical exploration of India’s human development trajectory in the context of Vision 2047.

Aims

This project examines India’s long-run human development trajectory in the context of the Government of India’s ambition to achieve high-income status by 2047. While India’s HDI has improved steadily since 1990, progress has lagged behind rapid income growth, raising concerns that India may become a high-income economy without attaining high human development.

Data

  • National HDI: UNDP HDRO API (automated)
  • State HDI: Global Data Lab (processed CSV)
  • Spending: RBI State Finances
  • State capacity: NITI Aayog Health & Education Indices
  • Labour & households: WPR and MPCE

Findings

PC1. India’s HDI trajectory since 1990

India’s HDI has improved steadily, but at a slower pace than income growth.

PC2. State HDI trajectories (interactive)

Large divergence across states despite similar income levels.

PC3–PC7. Explaining income-adjusted HDI

Tools & Methods

  • Python (pandas, sklearn)
  • Residual HDI framework
  • Standardised coefficients
  • Vega-Lite for charts
  • APIs for automation
  • Non-causal interpretation

Conclusions

  • Income growth alone is insufficient for high human development.
  • Household-level transmission mechanisms matter.
  • Vision 2047 requires employment-intensive and capability-led strategies.
