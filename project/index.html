PP434 Project

Pathways to High Human Development in India

(An empirical exploration of India’s human development trajectory in the context of Vision 2047)

Aims

This project examines India’s long-run human development trajectory in the context of the Government of India’s ambition to achieve high-income status by 2047 under the Vision 2047 agenda. Although India’s Human Development Index (HDI) has improved steadily since 1990, progress in human development has not kept pace with rapid income growth, raising concerns that India could become a high-income economy without attaining high human development. This project has two objectives. First, it documents India’s HDI progress over time and highlights the persistent divergence between economic expansion and improvements in health, education, and living standards. Second, it examines whether differences in policy priorities and state-level characteristics help explain why some Indian states achieve higher human development outcomes than would be predicted by income alone. Empirically, the analysis moves beyond simple income–HDI correlations and adopts a residual framework to assess income-adjusted human development performance across states.

Data

  • National HDI (1990–present): UNDP HDRO API (automated).
  • State HDI (1990–2023): Global Data Lab (processed into tidy CSV).
  • Health & education spending shares: RBI State Finances (compiled).
  • State capacity proxies: NITI Aayog Health Index; School Education Quality Index.
  • Labour/households: Workers–Population Ratio (WPR) and MPCE (where available).

Tools & methods

Workflow

  • Python (pandas/sklearn) for cleaning + regressions.
  • Residual approach: predict HDI from log(GNI), then analyse residuals.
  • Standardised betas (z-scores) for comparability across variables.

Challenges

  • Non-aligned years across indices (treated as proxies; noted).
  • Missing UT budget data (UTs dropped for spending models).
  • Cross-sectional associations only (no causal claims).
