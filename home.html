About me

I am a policy technocrat with over a decade of experience working at the intersection of public policy, human development, and state capacity in India. My work spans government, multilateral organisations, and civil society, with a focus on poverty measurement, SDG implementation, labour markets, and evidence-based policymaking.

I am a Chevening Scholar (2025-26) and currently pursuing the Master of Public Policy at LSE, where my academic focus includes causal inference, public management, political economy, and data-driven policy design.
Skills: Python (pandas, sklearn), Vega-Lite, causal inference, public finance, human development metrics, policy writing
