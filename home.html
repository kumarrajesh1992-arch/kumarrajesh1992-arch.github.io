About me

I am a policy technocrat with over a decade of experience across government institutions, multilateral organisations, and civil society in India—working at the intersection of human development, state capacity, and data-driven policymaking. My work has covered poverty measurement, SDG monitoring, labour market analysis, and evidence-based policymaking. I am currently a Chevening Scholar pursuing the Master of Public Policy at LSE.

Skills: Python (pandas, sklearn), Vega-Lite, data cleaning & reproducible workflows, causal inference foundations, public finance & budgeting, human development metrics (HDI/MPI), policy writing & government reporting.
